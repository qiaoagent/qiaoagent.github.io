In the Federal Budget

NIH named TrialGPT to Congress

Its Fiscal Year 2027 budget request names TrialGPT outright — in four separate documents.

Where it’s named

Three of the request’s volumes carry a distinct TrialGPT write-up; the fourth — the master overview — bundles them.

Executive Summary, p. 19
FY 2027 Budget — Executive Summary
Integrating AI Across Biomedical Research
“A team of researchers from NIH’s National Library of Medicine (NLM) and NCI harnessed the power of large language models to develop an innovative tool called TrialGPT… the research team was recently selected for The Director’s Challenge Innovation Award.”

Office of the Director, p. OD-19

Increasing clinical trial enrollment with TrialGPT
“The National Library of Medicine (NLM) developed a publicly accessible tool, TrialGPT… A user study revealed that TrialGPT can reduce the screening time by more than 42 percent in patient recruitment.”

FY 2027 Budget — Overall Appropriations
Improving access to clinical trials
“A team of NIH researchers harnessed the power of large language models to develop an innovative tool called TrialGPT that streamlines the clinical trial matching process… an annotated list of trials—ranked by relevance and eligibility—that clinicians can use to discuss clinical trial opportunities with their patient.”

FY 2027 Budget — Consolidated Overview

Why this is notable

Each year the President submits a budget request to Congress, and NIH accompanies it with a justification of its funding — a limited account of its mission and the scientific accomplishments it considers most representative. The audience is congressional appropriators, and space for specific projects is scarce.

Being named there — in the Executive Summary, alongside NIH’s flagship data-science and AI priorities — places TrialGPT among the handful of results NIH chose to represent its research to Congress for the coming fiscal year.
